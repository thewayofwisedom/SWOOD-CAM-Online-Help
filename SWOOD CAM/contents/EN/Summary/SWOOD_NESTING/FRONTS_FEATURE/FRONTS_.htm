FRONTS

The Fronts window is available in two ways:

  • By clicking on an Assembly in the Source files folder, then click on "Edit fronts".

  • By right-clicking on an Assembly in the Source files folder, then selecting "Edit fronts".

The fronts feature is available with a right click on an  Assembly in the Sources files folder and select "Edit Fronts":

 

WHAT'S THE FRONTS FEATURE ? 

The Fronts function allows to match the grain of the material on several contiguous fronts of the same cabinet. This function creates groups of fronts that will be nested as a unit.

INTERFACE

The Fronts window is composed of 2 sections:

Fronts list

The Front list shows the fronts in Automatic mode and those in Manual mode.

 Assembly: Selected assembly in the Nesting projects window.

                                   → By selecting the assembly, you can Reset the assembly.

            Automatic: SWOOD automatically detects groups of facades in the assembly according to certain rules.

                                   → By selecting the automatic mode, it is possible to do the following actions:

                                           - Enable/ Disable: Enables or disables the automatic mode.

                                           - Edit: Gives access to the calculation rules of the automatic mode:

                                                              -  Direction: Allows you to filter in which orientation a part will be recognized as a front. This orientation is based on the Z axis of the panel. The value of this parameter Direction corresponds to the opposite direction of Z axis of fronts as explained in the picture below (by default "All" is selected).

 

In the example above, in order for the selected part to be recognized as a front, the direction must be "Front".

                                                              -  Neighbors distance: Maximum distance between two parts to be combined in a front group.

In the example above, with parameter Distance set to 20mm, parts A and B will be recognized in the same group of fronts while parts C and D won't be grouped.

                                                              -  Use only facade condition: If this option is checked, only parts with the custom property called NESTING_FRONT, of text type and with value "Yes", will be included in the front group.

                                            -Unshow /Show potential parts: Hides or shows the parts that will be included in the front group in the Graphics Area.

                       Fronts: Front group detected by the automatic algorithm.

                                                    Front: Part that has been detected as a front by the automatic algorithm.

                                     → By selecting a front or a front group, in addition to being highlighted in the Graphics Area, it is possible to do the following actions:

                                                -  Add to manual: Transfers a automatically detected front or front group into the manual mode.

                                                -  Isolate: Graphically isolate the front or front group from the other parts in the Graphics Area.

           Manual: In the manual mode, it is possible to change the assignment of the fronts detected in the Automatic mode.

                       Fronts: Front group detected by the automatic algorithm and transferred to the manual mode.

                                                    Front: Part that has been defined as a front.

                            Part: Part that has been removed from the front list.

                                              → By selecting a front group, a front or a part, in addition to being highlighted in the Graphics Area, it is possible to do the following actions:

                                                     -  Remove from front (only for a front or a front group): Turn the front or front group into a part.

                                                     -  Create front (Only for parts): Turn a part into a front state.

                                                     -  Add to automatic: Transfer a front or a front group from manual mode to automatic mode.

                                                     -  Isolate: Graphically isolate the front or front group from the other parts in the Graphics Area.

 

Graphics Area

The Graphics Area allows to visualize the selected assembly in 3D with :

  • In yellow: The parts of the assembly that have been integrated into the front group.
  • In gray: The parts of the assembly that have not been integrated into the front group.
  • Reference Triad: Help to visualize the orientation of the 3D model. The Z axis is in blue, the X axis in red and the Y axis in green.

 

                    → By holding the left click (or wheel click) in the Graphics Area, it is possible to rotate the 3D model.

                   → By rotating the mouse wheel forward or backward, the Graphics Area zooms in on the pointer position.
