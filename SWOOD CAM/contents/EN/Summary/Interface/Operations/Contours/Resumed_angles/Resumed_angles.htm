RESUMED ANGLES

 

Introduction

Resumed angles is a milling to process the inner corners of a workpiece contour, usually contoured by a first Contour milling.

 

Selection

By selecting a face of a profile, you can resumed angles by clicking  on milling  or outline . A propagation window will appear on the screen, allowing you to manage the propagation of the selection :

  1.  Selection of the previous side: Propagates the selection to the next "leftmost" side of the selection
  2.  Selection of the next side: Propagates the selection to the next "rightmost" side of the selection
  3.  Full profile selection: Propagates the selection to all the profile.
  4.  Cancel the last selection: Cancels the previous propagation.
  5.  Validate the selection: Validates the propagation of the selection on which to apply the operation
  6.  Cancel selection: Cancels the whole selection

Once the tool and the Resumed angles milling have been selected, the operation configuration window will appear on the screen. 

  1.  Approach Z value
  2.  Extra thickness total
  3.  Maximum depth of passes
  4.  Exceeding (positive or negative values accepted)
  5.  Extra thickness lateral (positive or negative values accepted)

 

Machining after contour milling :

In order to resumed angles it is necessary to indicate the diameter of the tool previously used to contour the part. SWOOD will automatically detect the areas to be machined.

 

  1. Extra thickness roughing
  2. Diameter of the roughing tool
  3. Path lead in mm
  4. Path lead in % of diameter tool
